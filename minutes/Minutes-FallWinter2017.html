2. WCCBB-RES-2017-2: Amendment to More Clearly Define Roles of Executive Officers

Secretary Johnson stated that he wrote this resolution as well, and that it defines the officer roles. He stated that the resolution contains seven officer roles: Chair, Vice Chair, Secretary, Treasurer, Keeper of the Scroll, and two Officer-At-Large roles. Secretary Johnson stated that the college can vote on each role individually, or as a group. He said that he put names alongside those performing the roles as of now, but if we want to hold formal elections of office, that can be done, as well. Secretary Johnson stated that he has received notification of both Z. Voltin and P. Ough as being interested in the Officer-At-Large positions. He nominated them for those respective positions. Secretary Johnson also defined the Officer-At-Large role as one where tasks aren't as well-defined as other roles, but rather they will assist the other officers with tasks when they are overburdened.

Vice Chair Ness asked how many officer roles there are defined in the resolution.

Secretary Johnson responded that there are seven, but that that can be voted upon as well. He stated he wanted an odd number of officers.

Vice Chair Ness clarified that he was asking how many Officer-At-Large roles were defined in the resolution.

Secretary Johnson stated there are two.

W. Lovelace asked if the roles were administrative in nature. He clarified that what he wanted to know is whether the officer roles had special privileges regarding bringing motions to the table, or other things that they alone could do that normal members could not.

Secretary Johnson stated that all members of the College could bring motions to the table, and that the purpose of the officers was administrative in nature. He further clarified that the officers do not get additional votes, but are merely ways of assigning names to general, everyday tasks that need to be completed so that they get done in an orderly manner. Secretary Johnson said that no officer has any additional decision-making power, within this resolution, that other members of the College don't have.

Treasurer Breitwieser stated that any officer of an organization can be used to effect service, for the purposes of a legal dispute. He stated that this means that, if the Wu Chi Kung Fu Association were to be litigated against, serving notification of a lawsuit to any officer is effectively serving the notification to the organization. Treasurer Breitwieser stated that, for this reason, some organizations minimize the number of officer roles they choose to have in order to make serving lawsuits to the organization more difficult.

Secretary Johnson stated that, regardless, someone wanting to sue the organization would be able to find an officer to serve to. He said that just because an organization has fewer officer roles, it doesn't mean that they are less likely to have a suit filed against them.

Treasurer Breitwieser said that having fewer officer roles does not protect you from a lawsuit, but improper service can protect you from a lawsuit. He said that he had no issue with the number of officer roles, it's just something to consider before we make the decision to have additional Officer-At-Large positions. Treasurer Breitwiser stated that if one limits the avenues of proper service, it makes it more difficult to bring suit against the organization.

Treasurer Breitwiser stated that if we accept the officer roles, we need to make sure that every officer is aware that someone may serve them papers, and if so, they have to accept service and notify the organization immediately.

Vice Chair Ness inquired as to whether this means that every officer must accept service for lawsuites against each and every school underneath the association, or just lawsuits naming the association as a whole.

Treasurer Breitwieser responded that it depends on how the suit is written, but generally, it's just the association. He stated that, typically, lawsuits will start with individual schools first, and only move up to naming the association if it's something that they think is a grievance caused by the overarching organization.

Secretary Johnson inquired why Treasurer Breitwieser kept pointing to him when discussing lawsuits.

Treasurer Breitwieser stated that he thought it unlikely that the lawyer would be the one most likely to get sued, and Secretary Johnson happened to be sitting across from him.

Vice Chair Ness stated he would hide behind the lawyer unless there was gunfire.

P. Ferguson stated he would hide behind the lawyer especially if there was gunfire.

Secretary Johnson stated that this resolution should probably be fixed and voted upon during this meeting, since the organization is essentially in a limbo right now without clearly defined officer roles.

P. Ferguson inquired whether the roles were permenant or whether there would be regular elections for officer positions.

Secretary Johnson stated there are no term limits on officer roles. He stated that anyone can voluntarily give up their officer role, but the organization does not have regular elections on officer roles. Secretary Johnson stated that he thinks that having fresh blood and turnaround in officer roles is a good thing, but because our organization is small right now, it's more efficient if we have the same people doing the same jobs - it offers continuity.

P. Ferguson stated he agreed with Secretary Johnson. He thought that we didn't have enough people to replace those already in office, if there were regular elections.

W. Lovelace indicated he did not need to be an officer, but he said he was happy to help out if any of the officers needed assistance with their work.

Secretary Johnson asked the College whether anyone had any amendments to the resolution as it was written.

Treasurer Breitwieser pointed out that if the existing officers became overburdened, the College could entertain a motion to increase the number of officer roles at that time.

Motion

Secretary Johnson moved that the College of Black Belts approve WCCBB-RES-2017-2 as written.

Second

Seconded by Treasurer Breitwieser.

Vote

17-0-0; Motion Carried.

3. WCCBB-RES-2017-3: Rules for the Ryan Carden Memorial Tournament

Secretary Johnson stated that this resolution was actually originally inspired by R. Huus and that he just worded it.

R. Huus stated that the past few Ryan Carden Memorial Tournaments he's attended have had few, if any, references to Sifu Ryan Carden. He stated that he would prefer if there was more effort given toward educating new students about Sifu Ryan, since they didn't have the opportunity to get to know him, especially since the tournament is being conducted in his name. R. Huus stated that he felt that we should involve Ryan more in the tournament or that we should change the name of the tournament.

Secretary Johnson stated that he wanted to offer some clarification on how the resolution came to be worded. He stated that R. Huus and he talked about much of what R. Huus just mentioned, and this is reflected in the wording of the resolution, but that there were also a number of related issues that needed to be discussed. Secretary Johnson stated that the issues with miscommunication with this year's tournament and the organization surrounding this year's tournament made the event less effective, in his opinion, than it normally would be. He stated that it was his opinion that the head instructor of the school wishing to host the tournament for a given year must ask the College, in person, with no proxy requests allowed, to host the tournament.

Treasurer Breitwieser asked what would happen if noone asked to host the tournament.

Secretary Johnson stated that he hadn't considered that possibility.

P. Ferguson suggested that the College use a lottery system to decide which school would host the tournament should that situation arise.

Secretary Johnson stated that the College didn't have a rule on this prior to now. He argued that this resolution wouldn't be changing anything that was in place prior to now for this particular situation.

Treasurer Breitwieser asked if we change the rules to make it so someone has to ask, and noone asks, whether we would then not have a tournament for that year.

Secretary Johnson stated that perhaps the language of the resolution needs to be changed, but that the intention was to make it so someone else can't ask on your behalf. He gave the example that if he weren't at the meeting where this was discussed, R. Huus couldn't volunteer Secretary Johnson's school for the tournament.

W. Lovelace stated that if noone asks to host the tournament, he doesn't think that we should force anyone to do it. He stated that there will be back channels that aren't official anyway where people will be talking about this prior to the official meeting, so it's going to be known that there isn't a location for a given year.

Secretary Johnson stated that we can just add language to default to the Grand Forks school whenever there's a dispute.

R. Huus suggested that since the most active schools at this time were in the Twin Cities and Grand Forks, if noone volunteered, we could agree to host the tournament somewhere in between those two locations to make it convienient for all parties.

Treasurer Breitwieser looked affronted and said that he saw where this was heading, given that he was the only place 'in between' the Twin Cities and Grand Forks.

P. Ferguson stated that he wouldn't be in support of changing the name of the tournament. He stated that he thought it would be better to carve out specific time during the tournament to specifically honor Ryan and discuss his history with the current participants. P. Ferguson stated that taking away the name would be a dishonor to both Ryan and Dashi Gary, since this was the reason Dashi Gary started the tournament to begin with.

Secretary Johnson clarified that within the resolution is language stated very specifically that at least one event must involve Sifu Ryan Carden in some way.

R. Huus stated that he has concerns that as more and more generations of students become involved in the schools, and we don't specifically honor Sifu Ryan, then we get into a situation where noone knows why the tournament is named the way it is anymore except for those few 'old school' individuals still around that trained under Ryan. He said he was concerned that this would get lost once more as he believes it has.

Secretary Johnson said that this was no different from any other sport. He said that often in, for example, hockey, there will be an annual tournament that has the name of a child who died of cancer at an early age on the tournament. Secretary Johnson said that many of the individual participants don't know who that person was, they simply know they are coming to participate in a hockey tournament. He said that, often, the tournament directors will have a presentation at the start of the tournament explaining why the tournament is named the way it is, and that we should have something similar at the very least.

P. Ferguson stated that he thought that something like this at the beginning of the tournament might be a good idea for the new generations that didn't have the opportunity to learn from Sifu Ryan.

Treasurer Breitwieser stated that he agrees and that we should keep a tradition of honoring Ryan alive in order to educate the next generations of students about who came before them.

P. Ferguson stated that we need to mandate that every school that hosts/plans the tournament needs to have something with Ryan in it, as talking points about who he was, or techniques that they specifically learned from Ryan, or something similar.

W. Lovelace stated that he liked how the resolution was worded.

P. Ferguson agreed and said that he also thought the resolution was worded well.

Treasurer Breitwieser stated that when he was in law school, there was a memorial golf tournament named after someoone who was important to the law school. He said that they did a similar introduction to what Secretary Johnson talked about in his example, and that was the point of honoring them with the name of the tournament in that it forced everyone to consider who this person was and why they mattered. Treasurer Breitwieser stated that he didn't think that simply because we progress as an organization and expand that we need to change the name of the tournament due to the fact that more and more people no longer know who Ryan was.

P. Ferguson said that we'll have enough to pass on to future generations that came from Ryan that they don't necessarily need to have met him in person.

Treasurer Breitwiser said that he thought it was important to do this memorial ceremony every tournament because of that reason. He said that way, students will know where this knowledge came from, and who influenced those who influence them.

Vice Chair Ness said that he thought an idea brought up previously by Secretary Johnson with the goal of forming a committee made up of representatives of multiple schools to assist in planning the tournament each year was a good idea. He said he thought this would give consistency to the tournament events and ensure that items like the introduction and ceremonies aren't forgotten from year to year.

Secretary Johnson pointed out that the only challenge with having a committee organize an event like this is that there is a conflict that's generated if the committee is deciding what will be done, but the host school is financing the tournament. He said that every instructor is going to want to add their own flare and change the tournament to reflect their personality. Secretary Johnson said that he thought that if we moved toward a model where the committee dictated what the tournament content would be, then we should also move toward having the overarching organization finance the tournament as well.

P. Ferguson said that he thought that was fair. He said that one thing that could be done to resolve that potential conflict is to make the committee in charge of the more routine aspects of the tournament, such as items that are required to be included every year.

Secretary Johnson said that he designed this resolution to be flexible, and in his opinion, this is really the first step toward a model like what's been described. He said that the intent isn't to impose a bunch of rules on tournament hosts, but at the same time, to begin moving us toward a more structured and reproducible event each year.

W. Lovelace said that the committee doesn't need to dictate to the host school or instructor, but rather be guidance for what has worked or not worked at past events. He said that this offers continuity for future events. W. Lovelace said the committee could be giving advice, and it's up to the host school or instructor to take them or not.

Vice Chair Ness said he didn't think Ryan would want the tournament to be a hardship financially on whichever school was hosting it.

P. Ferguson agreed that Ryan would likely not want that.

Secretary Johnson said he didn't think it has been a hardship, but that this year, for example, there was an undue amount of pressure put on J. Stansbury. Secretary Johnson said that he didn't think J. Stansbury was expecting either the amount of pressure that he encountered, nor the lack of turnout that happened. He said that he thought what could be done to learn from this situation is to try to nail some of the details down more incrementally and further in advance so there would be less pressure closer to the date of the event itself. Secretary Johnson said that, while this is later in the meeting, if the Sinking Moon School hosts the tournament in 2018, he will commit to providing a continuity document stating what was done, when things were planned, what issues were encountered that were unexpected, and so forth. He said that this would be provided to each subsequent head instructor hosting the tournament so they can hopefully learn from each successive iteration to make the event better.

P. Ferguson stated he thought this was a good idea.

Z. Voltin stated that he agreed with P. Ferguson and W. Lovelace in the idea that we should have some formal document, either in the Constitution, By-laws, or some other permenant document, which defines the set of things that compose the 'Ryan Carden Memorial Tournament'. He said that this document should make it clear which events are required at each tournament that make it 'the' tournament. Z. Voltin also stated that while he agreed with Vice Chair Ness' idea of having a committee, he thought the committee should be advisory in nature only. He said that this committee would not have any power to enforce their will on the head instructor hosting the tournament, but that they were expected to advise the head instructor in matters related to the tournament, with the head instructor having the final say about what will or will not occur. Z. Voltin said this will provide a panel of experts that others who are less experienced with how to host an event like this can draw on, but they don't necessarily have to get approval for anything. He said that, in this way, every head instructor wouldn't need to become an 'expert' at the Ryan Carden Memorial Tournament, because they would have a panel of experienced people whom they could draw upon.

Secretary Johnson stated that he thought this was a really good idea.

Vice Chair Ness agreed that this was a good idea.

Secretary Johnson asked the College how they thought he should handle this. He said he thought this committee has a good place in the By-laws of the organization, and he can write it up as a separate resolution if others would like him to. He said we could amend the current resolution tonight if others felt that was in order.

W. Lovelace said that he thought the formation of a committee for this purpose was fine by itself as a standalone resolution unrelated to the one currently being discussed.

Secretary Johnson said that the College should plan on voting on that via email, since he would have to take some time to prepare the resolution.

Point of Order

Secretary Johnson said that, aside from a single vote tonight where Z. Voltin abstained, he didn't think there had been a single vote that hadn't been unanimous yet in the meetings of the College. Secretary Johnson said that if there is a vote where it appears unclear that it passed based on verbal vote to any member of the College, or if any member wanted to force a roll call vote, you can say 'Division', which will force a roll call vote to be taken, where each member present states their name and their vote for the record. He said the only time when this doesn't apply is if the Constitution or By-laws specifically call for a secret ballot vote, in which case, that would be the process regardless, and no verbal vote would be taken.

Motion

R. Huus moved to approve resolution WCCBB-RES-2017-3 as written.

Second

Seconded by P. Ferguson.

Vote

17-0-0; Motion Carried.
